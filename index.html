Colosseum, Italy | 7 Wonders of the World

An oval amphitheatre right in the heart of Rome, Colosseum is the largest standing amphitheatre in the world ever since it was inaugurated by the Flavian Emperor, Titus in 80 CE.   • History:The Colosseum, the idyllic symbol of Rome, does possess some interesting history! Emperor Vespasian built this vast structure during the Flavian dynasty.   • Significance:This 189 metres long amphitheatre is considered the largest in the world, possessing a section of the arena that was once under the ground. Talking about the significance of this towering structure is that the Colosseum stands as the symbol of power, genius, and brutality in Rome. This amphitheatre also served as a tool to the Roman Emperor for political control and showcasing their power.

  • Features: The elliptical shape of the Colosseum allows the people to enjoy the show in a seated circular form around the minor axis. It can easily have over fifty thousand people as an audience. The seats of this architectural masterpiece also acted as a support to the ascending levels.

  • Best Time To Visit: Colosseum can be best explored during the low season from November to February. During this time, the tourist crowds are fairly low compared to the rest of the year. Also, the opening and closing time of the Colosseum varies on the seasons throughout the year.
